Among other things, the scale of correlation of changes in the health condition of bank customers to the use of banks 'offer and the financial results of banks, the specificity of the sources of the health crisis and the demographic structure of the society taking into account the age, the level of resistance of the organism to certain diseases and diseases, the scale of citizens' use of certain types of banking services are important. etc. The impact of the current SARS-CoV-2 (Covid-19) pandemic on the commercial banking system was determined primarily by the introduced lockdown of the economy and the interventionist anti-crisis socio-economic policy instruments, including the so-called Anti-Crisis Shield consisting mainly in financial support of commercial enterprises from the funds of the state finance system. As the economic activity of enterprises decreased and the companies received non-returnable financial aid from the state to maintain business continuity, economic lending by economic entities decreased dramatically. On the other hand, the SARS-CoV-2 (Covid-19) coronavirus pandemic caused an increase in the digitization of economic processes, an increase in the Internet of settlements and payments, the development of e-commerce, etc. As a result, the turnover generated by online and mobile electronic banking is growing. However, the decline in loan revenue is greater than the revenue growth generated by the development of online electronic banking.
